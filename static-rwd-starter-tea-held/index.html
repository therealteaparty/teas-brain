About me: A very brief summary

I am an artist, a consumer, a collector, and sometimes a cook. I am writing this at 22 years old, which makes me the oldest of two siblings. That also means I’m nearing the end of my lifetime as a student.

As a baby child, I was born in Tampa, Florida. For the next few years, I moved around with my cool dad who played baseball and my beautiful mom until we finally settled in Indiana. I loved making lemonade stands, collecting (and eventually releasing) lizards, playing Tomodachi Life on my 2DS, and songwriting on a guitar that I had no idea how to play.

In middle school, I went through that phase where I listened to Twenty-One Pilots, wore chokers around my neck, silly bands on my wrists, and styled my hair with side-swept bangs.

By high school, I was primarily a show choir kid and a debate club member. On weekends, I would switch between my sparkly dresses and my suits. My mom says I'm really good at arguing because of my studies in Public Forum, which involved affirmative and negative arguments about hypothetical public polices. While I did earn fine scores, I once lost a round because the judge thought my hair should have been in a ponytail.

I loved my time at Indiana University, especially in the Media School and the Fine Arts Department. I’m glad that the Informatics degree has such interesting options through it’s cognates and specializations.
